Brewery profile

Daijiro

Hata Sake Brewery
Rich
Mellow
Fruity

Daijiro sake truly embodies the essence of Shiga Prefecture, with every aspect of production rooted deeply in the local environment. From the rice cultivated in the fields visible from the brewery to the hand-stained labels sourced from the town of Higashiomi, every detail reflects a commitment to locality and quality.

What sets Daijiro apart is the unique ambient yeast and bacteria present in the brewery, which impart distinctive characteristics to its kimoto and yamahai sake. The result is sake that is rich, bold, and powerful, with outstanding depth of flavor. Nama versions improve with age and showcase incredible depth when warmed.

Ambient microbes

The brewery's own ecosystem

The brewery boasts a distinct ecosystem of ambient lactic bacteria and yeasts. Using kimoto and yamahai starters, they produce sake with a rich flavor profile and elevated acidity. Each sake is released in kimoto, yamahai, and sokujo styles so customers can discern how starters shape flavor.

Rice

Three local varieties, visible from the door

Crafted from three locally-grown rice varieties: Ginfubuki and Watarebune, both native to Shiga, and Yamada Nishiki. The rice fields are visible from the brewery's entrance. Ginfubuki imparts umami richness. Watarebune contributes depth of flavor. Yamada Nishiki yields a drier, more subdued profile.

Muroka genshu

Unfined and undiluted — by principle

All Daijiro sake is both muroka (unfined) and genshu (undiluted). Hata-san opposes charcoal fining, viewing it as stripping sake of its inherent natural flavors. He also opts against dilution, so flavors remain robust and powerful — designed to pair with the preserved delicacies and renowned Omi Beef of Shiga.
