Brewery profile

Shinrai

Miwa Sake Brewery
Rich
Mellow
Fruity

We have not come across a better range and more balanced sake to pair with food than Shinrai. The brewery uses two sources of water, local rice, and innovative brewing techniques to counterbalance hard water and rich rice flavors with soft water and elegant brewing methods.

The result is a mix of styles with varying levels of richness and flavor that have a level of refinement and wonderful texture on the palate. Shinrai produces sake you don't get tired of drinking — they make wonderful sake for dinner parties.

Water

Two wells, two characters

Shinrai uses two distinct water sources. Soft water drawn from a 15-meter well is used within the brewery and during the final stage of fermentation — it creates the refined texture and smoothness. Hard water from a 50-meter well is used in the fermentation starter and first two additions. By combining the two, Shinrai achieves a harmonious balance: rich flavors with a smooth, elegant texture.

Rice

Jinsekikogen, expressed in every bottle

Miwa-san emphasizes expressing the region of Jinsekikogen through their sake. They use locally grown Sendbon Nishiki and Hattan Nishiki, both prefectural rice strains, with farm-direct contracts ensuring the sake embodies the essence of the region and its unique terroir.

Terroir

Kimoto, wood, and the brewery's own microflora

Miwa-san takes terroir further by looking to use the kimoto style in more production — relying on ambient lactic bacteria and yeast present in the brewery for a deeper connection to the local environment. He also incorporates local wood to construct wooden fermentation vessels, a practice reminiscent of sake brewing prior to the 20th century.
