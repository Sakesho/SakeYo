Beach Road · Profile

The chef at OMU NOMU quit law to pour sake nobody else here is pouring

Gerard Alexis — Alex — runs a counter room on Beach Road built around the sake most Singapore lists won't touch. Muroka nama genshu. Koshu aged for years. Doburoku, which he was the first in the city to pour seriously.

Tanjong Pagar · Profile

The man who warms sake by hand in a city that never gets cold

Shukuu Izakaya is the kind of room four friends build when they miss Tokyo after work. Mario Choy is one of them — chef-partner, trained designer, the person who drew the charcoal Great Wave along the back wall. Ask him why he warms sake in a country that never needed a warm drink.

Industry · Profile

Adrian Goh was importing sake into Singapore before the city had a scene

He started in 2013. Twelve years and two hundred brewery visits later, he runs Inter Rice Asia, co-founded Sake Matsuri, and in 2024 became the first Singaporean named a Sake Samurai. If you drink good sake in this city, there's a decent chance he's the reason it's here.
