The taxonomy

Style by style

01
Nigori
The cloudy one. Unfiltered or coarsely filtered, with rice lees left in the bottle.
Read
02
Seasonal sake
Shinshu in winter, hiyaoroshi in autumn — the few sake with a season printed on the label.
Read
03
Nama
Unpasteurized, slightly alive. Ships cold, changes in the bottle, rewards you for chasing it.
04
Koshu
Aged sake — sometimes a decade old. Amber, honeyed, the closest thing to sherry in Japan.
05
Ginjo style
Polished rice, fragrant yeast, low-temperature fermentation. The aromatic, pear-and-melon category.
06
Kijoshu
Brewed with sake in place of water in the final addition. Rich, sweet, built for dessert pairings.
07
Taruzake
Aged briefly in cedar barrels. Picks up the forest — resinous, crisp, long tied to celebration.
08
Sparkling
Bottle-fermented or carbonated. Two methods, and the short list of brewers getting it right.
09
Fresh press
Arabashiri, nakadori, seme — the three draws from a single pressing, each tasting a little different.
